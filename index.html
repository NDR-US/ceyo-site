ACCOUNTABILITY GAP

Telemetry describes activity.
It does not preserve proof.

When decisions are disputed — regulatory inquiry, internal audit, incident review, litigation — organizations reconstruct events from partial logs and traces. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

Institutions need artifacts that are canonical in structure, explicit in capture scope, cryptographically sealed, and independently verifiable — without default exposure of proprietary internals.

01 / CANONICAL
DETERMINISTIC STRUCTURE
⌄

Stable schema and stable bytes across environments. Verification does not depend on logging format or interpretive reconstruction.

02 / SEALING
HASH + SIGNATURE
⌄

Hashing establishes tamper-evidence. Signatures establish origin authenticity under operator-controlled key custody.

03 / SCOPE
POLICY-DEFINED DISCLOSURE
⌄

Policy defines inclusion, exclusion, and masking before generation. Disclosure boundaries are explicit, versioned, and reviewable.

04 / SEPARATION
RECORDS, NOT JUDGMENTS
⌄

Verification is mechanical. Interpretation is institutional. Evidence infrastructure must remain separate from decision authority.

EVIDENTIARY SUBSTRATE

Decoupled verification architecture.

CEYO attaches at the inference boundary (sidecar, gateway, or wrapper). It captures only what policy defines, normalizes the context into a canonical artifact, then seals it cryptographically.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. It does not confirm correctness of model output.
