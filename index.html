GO BEYOND TELEMETRY

CEYO is a weight-neutral, policy-scoped integrity layer that attaches beside AI inference systems and produces canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context — cryptographically sealed for institutional verification under constrained disclosure.

ENTER FRAMEWORK REQUEST CONTROLLED REVIEW
BOUNDARY Records, not judgments. Integrity, not correctness. Verification under policy scope.

Telemetry describes activity. It does not preserve proof.

Most AI deployments optimize for throughput, latency, and availability. Accountability infrastructure is frequently secondary. When decisions are disputed — regulatory inquiry, internal audit, incident review, or litigation — organizations reconstruct events from partial logs and traces. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic proof.

CANONICAL STRUCTURE

Deterministic schema and stable byte representation across environments.

EXPLICIT SCOPE

Policy-defined inclusion, exclusion, and masking rules recorded at capture time.

CRYPTOGRAPHIC SEALING

Hashing and signatures provide tamper-evidence and origin authenticity.

INDEPENDENT VERIFICATION

Integrity can be validated without default exposure of proprietary internals.

Position: this is an architectural deficit, not an operational oversight.

A decoupled evidentiary substrate.

CEYO attaches at the inference boundary (sidecar, gateway, or wrapper). It captures only what policy defines, normalizes captured context into a canonical artifact, applies deterministic canonicalization (RFC 8785), and seals the artifact cryptographically.

Core principle: evidence infrastructure must remain separate from decision authority.

CEYO provides the artifact · Institutions provide the oversight
