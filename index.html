Neutral evidentiary layer · Policy-scoped capture · Independent verification

Evidentiary infrastructure for AI-supported systems.

CEYO is a conceptual evidentiary infrastructure for AI-supported decision systems.

It produces policy-scoped, deterministic, cryptographically sealed records of AI inference events. These records can be independently verified by any party who holds the sealed artifact and the corresponding public key — without access to model weights, proprietary internals, or the contents of the underlying decision.

CEYO operates outside the model. It does not modify, instrument, or interfere with AI inference. It records only what a governing capture policy explicitly permits.

Objective

Why this exists.

AI systems increasingly influence consequential outcomes: credit, employment, healthcare, benefits, autonomous control. In regulated and high-stakes contexts, there is a distinct requirement that cannot be met by internal logging alone: the ability to demonstrate that a system behaved as declared, to an external party, without disclosing what it decided.

  • CEYO addresses this gap.
  • A sealed artifact proves that a record was produced at a specific time, under a specific policy, and has not been altered since — without revealing what the underlying system processed or concluded.
  • The focus is evidentiary integrity, not operational dependency.
Core mechanics

Record · Seal · Verify

CEYO operates as a neutral evidentiary layer attached to an AI inference boundary. It captures policy-scoped decision records and produces deterministic, cryptographically sealed artifacts that can be independently verified.

Record

Policy-scoped capture

Recording only fields permitted by an explicit, declared capture policy.

Seal

Deterministic canonicalization + cryptographic integrity

Serialization that produces identical bytes for identical inputs, followed by hashing and digital signatures that make post-hoc alteration detectable.

Verify

Independent verification

Recomputing the hash and validating the signature using only the artifact and a public key.

Five steps

The mechanics chain is fixed.

Each step produces a verifiable boundary.

Operational sequence

01 · Attach

Deploy as a sidecar, gateway, or wrapper at the inference boundary.

02 · Capture

Define scope first. Policy-scoped fields only. Masking applied before sealing.

03 · Canonicalize

Normalize deterministically so identical inputs produce identical serialized bytes.

04 · Seal

Apply hashing and digital signatures under operator-controlled key custody.

05 · Verify

Recompute canonicalization, recompute hashes, validate signatures, and confirm policy alignment.

How it works

Evidence path at a glance.

Every CEYO artifact passes through a fixed lifecycle.

Step What happens
Policy-scoped capture An inference event is recorded within the bounds of a declared capture policy; out-of-scope fields are excluded by design.
Deterministic canonicalization The captured body is serialized using RFC 8785, producing identical bytes for identical inputs regardless of key ordering or whitespace.
Cryptographic sealing A SHA-256 hash and an ECDSA P-256 signature are computed over the canonical bytes.
Independent verification Any party with the sealed artifact and the corresponding public key can recompute the hash and validate the signature, with no access to the originating system.
Boundary statement

What CEYO does not claim.

CEYO is an evidentiary infrastructure. It is not:

Not a truth engine

CEYO does not verify that an AI decision was correct.

Not a fairness engine

CEYO does not assess whether a decision was equitable or unbiased.

Not a compliance certification

CEYO does not certify conformance with any regulatory standard.

  • CEYO does not access, analyze, or evaluate model internals.
  • CEYO proves that a sealed record is unaltered and was produced under a declared policy.
  • It does not prove anything about the quality, legality, or appropriateness of the underlying AI output.
