Company · Principles · Responsible deployment

Evidence infrastructure for AI systems.

CEYO exists to close a repeatable oversight gap: most inference deployments retain outputs and logs, but lack canonical, integrity-sealed records designed for independent verification under constrained disclosure.

What we build

CEYO is an attachable evidentiary layer at the inference boundary. It produces policy-scoped audit artifacts through a fixed chain: capture → canonicalize → seal → disclose → verify.

  • Deterministic canonicalization (RFC 8785) for reproducible hashing
  • Cryptographic sealing for tamper-evidence and provenance checks
  • Non-custodial key posture (operator-controlled keys)
  • Tiered disclosure for review without default IP exposure

Principle

Evidence infrastructure must remain separate from decision authority.

CEYO produces records. Institutions and authorized operators make determinations. Verification confirms integrity and provenance under policy scope — not correctness, legality, or compliance conclusions.

Operating principles

A posture designed for institutional review and constrained disclosure.

NeutralityNon-adjudication

We do not certify compliance or decision correctness. We structure records so institutions can review.

VerifiabilityDeterminism

Canonicalization and sealing enable repeatable, mechanical verification outcomes (PASS/FAIL/POLICY_MISMATCH).

Minimal disclosureTiered access

Policy scope and disclosure tiers define what is visible before generation. No implicit expansion after the fact.

Operator controlNon-custodial keys

Signing keys remain in operator HSM/KMS/TEE. CEYO is designed not to possess private keys.

How we engage

A review-first workflow, aligned to oversight needs.

Evaluation pathway

  • Public layer: high-level posture, samples, and boundaries.
  • Controlled review: specs, capture policy schema, verification harness details (under NDA/terms).
  • Deployment: operator-defined policy scope, key custody framework, retention posture.

What CEYO is not

  • Not a model evaluation or safety certification body
  • Not a compliance determination engine
  • Not an operational command or monitoring system
  • Not a guarantee of legal admissibility or regulatory sufficiency
