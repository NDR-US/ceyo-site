About

CEYO is evidence infrastructure for AI systems.

CEYO exists to close a repeatable oversight gap in modern AI deployment: most inference systems retain outputs and operational logs, yet lack canonical, integrity-sealed records designed for independent verification when outcomes are disputed.

CEYO is not an AI platform and it is not a decision system. It does not evaluate correctness, legality, fairness, or policy compliance. CEYO produces records. Authorized institutions and operators make determinations.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope — not correctness of model output.
What CEYO builds

CEYO attaches at the inference boundary and generates a structured audit artifact through a fixed mechanics chain: capture → canonicalize → seal → disclose → verify. Capture scope is policy-defined. Canonicalization produces deterministic bytes (RFC 8785) for reproducible hashing. Sealing applies cryptographic hashing and digital signatures so tampering is detectable and provenance can be validated. Disclosure tiers support verification without default exposure of proprietary internals.

Operating posture

CEYO is designed for institutional review. It prioritizes determinism, scope discipline, and constrained disclosure. Signing keys remain in operator-controlled environments (HSM/KMS/TEE). CEYO is non-custodial by design: it does not possess, transmit, or store private signing keys.

CEYO does not certify regulatory compliance or legal admissibility. Suitability and sufficiency are determined by the relevant authority.

How CEYO is evaluated

Public materials are intended to show structure and boundaries: sample artifacts, verification outcomes (PASS/FAIL), and a preview of verification steps. Controlled materials (under appropriate terms) include detailed specifications, capture policy schema, and verification harness documentation.

Responsible deployment

CEYO is built to support accountability, not circumvent it. Deployments must be authorized and lawful, with capture scope proportionate to oversight purpose. CEYO does not replace institutional review, legal review, operational command authority, or human authorization controls in consequential contexts.
